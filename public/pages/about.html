News built for the whole family.

Skynet Nexus News is a daily news network for readers ages 5–50. Every story is written so parents and kids can read it together — with a Kid Take summary, a Family Discussion prompt, and a glossary of new words. Free forever, no ads targeted at kids.

What we cover

Four beats. One mission: real youth achievement, told with respect for both curious 8-year-olds and their parents. If it happens in a lab, a robot pit, a creative workshop, or on a festival stage — and the humans making it happen are under 25 — it belongs on Skynet.

Our values

Family-first, always. Content is designed to work as bedtime reading, breakfast-table conversation starters, and independent reading for confident readers alike.

Kid-safe by design. Every article passes a content guardrail. No violence, no politics, no drama for drama's sake. If we can't write about it in a way that respects both ages, we don't run it.

Primary sources only. When possible, we link papers, source code, official competition footage, and artist pages — not press-release recaps.

No hype cycles. No VC drama, no influencer noise, no chasing viral moments.

Free forever. Everything stays free. No paywalls, no dark patterns, no ads tracking your kids.

How to read with your kid

Every article has three sections designed for co-reading:

1. The full story — written for adults and older kids (roughly middle-school reading level).

2. The Kid Take — 2-3 sentences at roughly age-8 reading level. Great for reading aloud to younger kids.

3. Family Discussion — two or three open-ended questions to talk about together. No right answers, just prompts.

Sign in and add a kid profile to get age-appropriate filtering — stories tagged 12+ will hide when a younger kid is browsing.
